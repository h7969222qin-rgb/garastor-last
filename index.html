The Three Collections

Three Forms · One Philosophy

Like the great design traditions of Italy — from the scholastic clarity of Aquinas to the cyclical vision of Vico, from the harmonious proportions of Alberti to the Baroque tension of Borromini — GARASTOR arranges its floors into three distinct collections. Each is a different meditation on form, space, and the relationship between the material and the divine. A different way for a room to breathe. A different memory carved into oak.

La Via Diritta · The Straight Path

Straight Plank

Where the line becomes liturgy

The clarity of Aquinas — every plank a proposition, every grain a syllogism toward truth Alberti's harmonies — proportion becomes prayer, the floor a foundation for the contemplative life The Augustinian path — straight lines leading the eye and the soul toward the uncreated light
La Freccia Divina · The Divine Arrow

Chevron

Points that point beyond themselves

The Vichian spiral — history doesn't repeat, it ascends in ever-widening arcs Borromini's tension — the dynamic line that reaches, the Baroque refusal of stasis Dante's ascent — each chevron a step in the Purgatorial climb toward perfection
Il Cosmo Intrecciato · The Woven Cosmos

Herringbone

Interlaced order, infinite continuity

The Franciscan weave — all creation interlocked, every being touching every other being Leonardo's knot — the infinite complexity within perfect geometry, the cosmos as tessellation Croce's spirit — history as living pattern, the floor a palimpsest of every step ever taken upon it

The Art of the Floor

Every Plank,
A Meditation on Matter

Superficie · The Surface

As Aquinas distinguished essence from existence, so we distinguish surface from soul. The hand-brushed, smoked, and carbonized finishes are the first encounter — the moment before understanding, pure sensation. Your feet decide in 0.3 seconds.

Anima · The Soul

In the Aristotelian tradition inherited by the Italian universities, every substance has its form. European oak from managed forests, each species carries its own entelechy — its own drive toward the perfection of its nature. This is the longest-lasting experience.

Eternità · The Eternal

Like Augustine's City of God, a true floor must outlast its makers. We select only FSC-certified, 50-year+ mature forests. The material returns to dust, but the form it held — that persists. Every footprint leaves no trace; every floor leaves a mark on time itself.
